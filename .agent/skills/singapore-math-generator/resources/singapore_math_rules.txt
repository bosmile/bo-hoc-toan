# Singapore Math Core Rules

## 1. CPA Approach (Concrete-Pictorial-Abstract)
- **Concrete**: Use physical objects to understand concepts.
- **Pictorial**: Translate objects into visual representations like drawings or bar models.
- **Abstract**: Use numbers and mathematical symbols (+, -, ×, ÷) to solve the problem.

## 2. Bar Modeling Principles
- **Part-Whole Model**: Used for addition and subtraction. Visualizes how parts combine to form a whole.
- **Comparison Model**: Used to compare two or more quantities. Helpful for "more than" or "less than" problems.
- **Equal Parts Model**: Used for multiplication and division. Shows multiple equal blocks representing the same value.

## 3. Problem Solving Protocol
- **Step 1: Understand**: Read the problem carefully. Identify the knowns and unknowns.
- **Step 2: Plan (Model)**: Draw a Bar Model to visualize the relationships between numbers.
- **Step 3: Solve**: Perform the calculations based on the model.
- **Step 4: Check**: Review the answer for logical consistency and accuracy.

## 4. Key Terminology
- **Sum**: Total of parts.
- **Difference**: The gap between two quantities in a comparison model.
- **Product**: Total value of multiple equal blocks.
- **Quotient**: Value of one block when the total is divided.
